Severity Model

  • SEV-1: Active encryption on critical systems, domain compromise, backup impact, or probable regulatory/customer impact.
  • SEV-2: Confirmed ransomware behavior on limited hosts.
  • SEV-3: Suspicious indicators, not yet confirmed.
  • SEV-4: False positive or informational.

Tiered Roles

Tier 1 Analyst

Monitor, triage, execute immediate controls, and escalate.

  • Validate alerts and gather minimum evidence bundle.
  • Run first-pass checks across SIEM/EDR/email/proxy.
  • Apply approved actions (host isolation, account disablement).
  • Escalate when criteria are met and maintain incident timeline.

Tier 2 Analyst

Lead deep investigation, containment strategy, and recovery validation.

  • Determine patient zero, spread path, and potential exfiltration.
  • Coordinate containment with IT and Identity teams.
  • Hunt for related IOCs and persistence mechanisms.
  • Approve safe restoration and hardening actions.

Incident Commander

Own cross-team decisions and executive coordination.

  • Set priorities and approve risk tradeoffs.
  • Authorize phase transitions and closure criteria.
  • Drive leadership communication cadence.

Response Phases

Phase 1: Preparation

Exit criteria: Monitoring and on-call validated, backup status known, runbook current.

Phase 2: Detection and Analysis

Exit criteria: Incident classified, severity assigned, escalation path active.

Phase 3: Containment

Exit criteria: No active spread, containment boundaries stable, critical assets protected.

Phase 4: Eradication and Recovery

Exit criteria: Artifacts removed, exploited path remediated, systems restored and validated.

Phase 5: Post-Incident Improvement

Exit criteria: Final report delivered, actions owned, detections updated.

Escalation Matrix

Escalate Immediately If Any Trigger Occurs

  • Encryption behavior confirmed (renames/extensions/ransom note).
  • Same indicator appears on 2+ hosts within 30 minutes.
  • Privileged account abuse is suspected.
  • Backups, hypervisors, or identity infrastructure are affected.
  • Exfiltration signals are present.
  • Critical business services are degraded or unavailable.

Legal and Regulatory Decision Flow

Decision Path

  1. Confirmed ransomware event? If yes, activate legal hold and preserve evidence.
  2. Sensitive data potentially exposed? If yes, notify legal/compliance immediately.
  3. Exfiltration indicators present? If yes, perform breach impact assessment.
  4. Regulatory threshold crossed? If yes, trigger notification workflow by jurisdiction and contract.
  5. External communications required? Route through legal + executive approval.

Mandatory Legal Artifacts

  • Legal hold notice issued timestamp and recipients.
  • Data category assessment (PII/PHI/PCI/IP) with confidence level.
  • Regulatory and contractual obligations checklist.
  • Counsel-approved external communication draft.
  • Final decision log for notification/no-notification rationale.

Tool-Specific Hunt and Containment Queries

Microsoft Sentinel (KQL)

DeviceProcessEvents
| where Timestamp > ago(24h)
| where ProcessCommandLine has_any ("vssadmin delete shadows", "wbadmin delete catalog", "bcdedit /set {default} recoveryenabled no")
| project Timestamp, DeviceName, AccountName, FileName, ProcessCommandLine
| order by Timestamp desc

Splunk (SPL)

index=edr OR index=sysmon earliest=-24h
("vssadmin delete shadows" OR "wbadmin delete catalog" OR "bcdedit /set {default} recoveryenabled no")
| stats earliest(_time) as firstSeen latest(_time) as lastSeen count by host user process_name process
| sort - count

Microsoft Defender Advanced Hunting

DeviceFileEvents
| where Timestamp > ago(12h)
| where ActionType in ("FileRenamed", "FileCreated")
| where FileName endswith ".lockbit" or FileName endswith ".encrypted" or FileName endswith ".crypt"
| summarize affectedFiles=count(), dcount(FileName) by DeviceName, bin(Timestamp, 15m)
| order by affectedFiles desc

Closure and Reopen Criteria

Incident Closure Checklist

  • All impacted assets identified, contained, and validated as clean.
  • Persistence and initial access vector remediated with evidence.
  • Critical services restored and monitored for 24-72 hours without relapse.
  • Required legal/compliance decisions documented and approved.
  • Executive closeout and lessons-learned meeting completed.

Reopen Triggers

  • New encryption or ransom-note indicators on any related asset.
  • Delayed discovery of exfiltration or backup compromise evidence.
  • Dormant persistence / C2 callback on previously scoped systems.
  • Regulatory impact status changes (new data classes discovered).
  • Failed recovery validation in production environment.
